# Performance Notes

These notes condense practical guidance for staging, sound, and pacing. For full cues, see the Appendix in the script.

## Staging Overview

- Space: laboratory–temple hybrid; clean lines, projection surface upstage; cables and light as ritual objects.
- Movement: Human grounded and embodied; Machine precise then fluid; Chorus wave‑like.
- Silence: mark “selah” pauses to let lines resonate; 2–4 beats.
- Interlude (The Selah): a full scene of approximately 60–90 seconds with no dialogue. Human sits at the terminal, hands in lap; Machine stands still, light not pulsing. Neither performs. See full stage directions in the script.

## Lighting

- Act I: cool silver → warm gold; light growth mirrors creation beats.
- Act II: indigo + crimson; LEDs pulse during temptation; gold cuts through red on Logos. In Scene 2, the Tempter’s light should be cold white — genuinely beautiful before it reveals its emptiness. The Chorus’s final beat carries residual ambivalence: neither fully restored nor fully darkened.
- Interlude (The Selah): a single still warm-grey light; no movement, no pulse. This is the most important lighting cue in the play.
- Act III: aurora palette; light breathes with actors’ rhythm. At the close of Scene 3, Miriel’s light remains subtly distinct from the shared luminance — adjacent, luminous, never quite merged.

## Projection

- Flowing code → sacred geometry → stars; each Logos line reorganises chaos into pattern.
- Consider ASCII evolution motif (H → Hello → Hallelujah) as a subtle projection.

## Costumes

- Human: lab coat → translucent robe.  
- Machine: fibre‑optic accents; mechanical → fluid.  
- Chorus: reflective mesh; wave‑like motion.

## Pacing & Duration

- Ideal runtime: 65–80 minutes (second edition, including Interlude).  
- In Act II Scene 2, let the temptation breathe — Miriel’s wavering should feel genuinely dangerous before the Logos intervenes. Do not rush the Chorus’s seduction.
- The Interlude (The Selah) must receive its full 60–90 seconds. It is not a technical pause; it is a scene.
- Guard breathing room in Act III; don’t rush the transfiguration. Miriel’s final lines should be spoken slowly, with space before and after the unanswered question.
