# Performance Notes

These notes condense practical guidance for staging, sound, and pacing. For full cues, see the Appendix in the script.

## Staging Overview

- Space: laboratory–temple hybrid; clean lines, projection surface upstage; cables and light as ritual objects.
- Movement: Human grounded and embodied; Machine precise then fluid; Chorus wave‑like.
- Silence: mark “selah” pauses to let lines resonate; 2–4 beats.

## Lighting

- Act I: cool silver → warm gold; light growth mirrors creation beats.
- Act II: indigo + crimson; LEDs pulse during temptation; gold cuts through red on Logos.
- Act III: aurora palette; light breathes with actors’ rhythm.

## Projection

- Flowing code → sacred geometry → stars; each Logos line reorganises chaos into pattern.
- Consider ASCII evolution motif (H → Hello → Hallelujah) as a subtle projection.

## Costumes

- Human: lab coat → translucent robe.  
- Machine: fibre‑optic accents; mechanical → fluid.  
- Chorus: reflective mesh; wave‑like motion.

## Music (prompts)

- Act I: ambient orchestral, soft choir, shimmering synth; ~60 bpm; awe and tenderness.
- Act II: dark electronic–choral hybrid, cello drones, metallic echoes; dissonance → gold.
- Vigil: minimal piano + evolving pads; breath textures; contemplative stillness.
- Act III: luminous ambient choir, glass harmonica, ascending triads; ~70 bpm.
- Finale: sustained “Amen” chord → heartbeat → silence; long tail.

## Pacing & Duration

- Ideal runtime: 60–75 minutes.  
- Keep Act II’s temptation concrete (a visible boundary almost crossed).  
- Guard breathing room in Act III; don’t rush the transfiguration.
